💼 The Company

O-PitBlast is a technology company focused on mining and explosives, providing tools and functionalities that assist in the efficient management and execution of mining operations, especially with regard to the planning and execution of controlled blasting.

💻 The Software

Surface: O-Pit Surface works in open-cast mines and quarries, allowing you to maintain total control over the results of explosive applications, accurately predict vibrations and efficiently manage fragmentation results, all on a unified and advanced platform.

The primary function of mining and explosives software is to provide tools and functionalities that assist in the efficient management and execution of mining operations, especially with regard to the planning and execution of controlled blasts.

🔍 Analysis

The Problem

The company had been using the software for more than 6 years, so the software's processing capacity was quite outdated.

The company had new competitors on the market who were a few steps ahead of their product. In addition, the mining-focused technology was constantly evolving and certain old features limited their ability to meet current customer needs.

The Solution

Navigation Improvements: Improve product navigation, making it easier for users to find what they are looking for. Reorganization of menus, the addition of advanced search functionalities and the creation of a more intuitive information structure.

Make it clean: Simplify the user interface to make it more intuitive and easier to use. Removing unnecessary elements and simplifying complex workflows can significantly improve the user experience.

Methodology

In the context of a product focused on mining and explosives and working within an agile methodology, my role as UX is to ensure that the user experience meets the needs of the professionals and the objectives of the business. This includes analyzing requirements and behavior to understand their needs, challenges and project objectives.

Creating user-centered designs and collaborating closely with cross-functional teams, including developers, product managers and other stakeholders to continuously test and iterate and consider security issues. Always participate in daily stand-up meetings, sprint reviews and continuous collaboration.

                🔑 Key Insights

                ⚠️ Systemic Issues and a Lack of Coordination in Communication (beyond UX)

                A significant proportion of calls were not related to service failures, but rather to logistical issues and, above all, to communications from other channels, such as TV campaigns or digital advertising.

                For example, whenever there were ticket sales campaigns (e.g. for events or concerts) directed to the same contact line, the call centre would quickly become overwhelmed with requests from across the country. As a result, critical enquiries, particularly from customers awaiting information on deliveries of large household appliances, were left on hold for extended periods or went unanswered.

                This scenario highlighted a structural flaw: the lack of segmentation of contact channels by service type. By centralising different needs on a single line, the Marketing department ended up placing unnecessary pressure on an already fragile operation, compromising the quality of the service provided.

                In short, the problem was not just one of UX, but of strategic misalignment between Marketing, Operations and Logistics, with a direct impact on the customer experience and the efficiency of customer service.

                🕳️Delivery Gaps for Large Household Appliances

                Delivery failures exposed a critical issue in the customer experience, with a direct impact on the contact centre.

                • Uncompleted deliveries led to repeated customer enquiries

                • The lack of tracking increased uncertainty and anxiety

                • Communication based on estimates created mismatched expectations

                With no visibility of where their order was, customers relied on the call centre. When deliveries frequently failed, customers would become frustrated after missing a day’s work waiting for their large household appliance to arrive, leading to a loss of trust and increased pressure on operations.

                An end-to-end failure, where a lack of transparency and control amplifies the impact of logistical problems.

                • Lack of technology to inform the customer where their order is

                • Out-of-date information between systems

                • Difficult rescheduling

                • Unrealistic promises

                👉 Result: failure → uncertainty → contact → overload → poor experience

                🛑 Problems throughout the journey

                From purchase to delivery, created a domino effect that had a direct impact on the customer experience and operations.

                The process began with a purchase based on trust in the brand, but without clear guarantees of tracking. The customer was unsure whether they would receive a working tracking number The website featured ambiguous communication, creating unclear expectations

                From there, any operational failure quickly snowballed:

                👉 Purchase with little clarity → Poorly defined expectations → Delivery failure → Customer without information attempts to contact the contact center → Increase in calls → Agent with limited or out-of-date data → Handling time increases → Queue grows → More customers affected → Experience deteriorates at scale

                🌡️ Weather Factors

                A clear pattern emerged: Heatwaves (summer) and heavy rainfall (autumn) disrupted logistics operations The drivers’ contract terms limited operational flexibility

                👉 Result: undelivered parcels → immediate increase in customer enquiries

                📊 CSD - Understanding

                During meetings with sales staff who have been with the company for almost a decade, the ux team and the product team, the certainties were the guaranteed information and feelings that users have.

                Doubts were uncertainties to be resolved that the customer or future customer has towards the software.

                Assumptions are predictions based on previous experiences, such as: at trade fairs where the product is sold, what were the biggest questions future customers had before purchasing the software. This way we can mitigate risks and make more informed decisions during the planning and execution of the project in question.

                👷🏻‍♂️ Who uses?

                Mining Engineers, Geologists, Mining Technicians and Operations Managers are among the professionals who rely on blasting software for their activities.

                The user journey of this software involves an integration of planning, execution, analysis and collaboration, ensuring the success of operations in open-pit mines.

                The use of blasting software plays a crucial role at every stage of the process.

                👣 The Journey

                The user journey when using blasting software involves several essential steps for the safe planning and execution of mining operations. Such as importing topographical data of the area of interest, defining the location and depth of the drill holes where the explosives will be inserted, explosive charge in each drill hole, specifying the type of explosive, quantity and charge pattern to optimize blasting effectiveness.

                ⏱ Timing

                One of the most important crucial points is undoubtedly Timing, which is where the user can predict what will happen, and this process needs a great deal of error prevention.

                Timing involves controlling the time between detonations by programmable electronic devices, such as electronic detonators, allowing precise synchronization and fine adjustments, or by mechanical or non-electronic devices, such as safety fuses or mechanical initiation systems, which is less precise than electronic timing.

                🧪 Simulating

                With the software, the user can simulate the speed of dismantling, allowing a detailed and precise analysis of the explosion process in different scenarios and conditions.

                This functionality gives the user the ability to adjust and optimize the blasting speed to meet the specific needs of the project, taking into account factors such as the type of explosive, the depth of the drill holes, the geological characteristics of the terrain and safety requirements.

                By simulating the blasting speed, the user can identify potential problems or areas for improvement, make informed decisions and ensure efficient and safe execution of blasting operations.
