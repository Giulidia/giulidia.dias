💼 The Company

Neon is a fintech with the mission of combining technology and design to redesign and simplify people's financial experience and help them manage their money better.

🔍 Analysis

🧨 Problem

Chargeback is the return of the sales transaction to the issuer as a result of a disputed purchase due to fraud or commercial disagreement. The process of contesting purchases was often not clear enough for customer service employees and they didn't had a place to support them while they were talking to the client about how the chargeback process works, This is a process that needs more attention when the information is passed on to the end customer.

💡 The Solution

I established the flow for the customer success team, from the moment the purchase is reported in the product application, so that the customer understands how the basic process works and if the customer has any further questions about how the debit card chargeback process works and wants to contact customer service, the team can explain it easily, with a lot of empathy, and say what the team should explain to the customer.

📚 Methodology

As a Business Analyst and this role falls under the UX umbrella, my role was to collaborate with cross-functional teams to define requirements, carry out research and develop flows, prioritize tasks for short development cycles and deliver them interactively.

Always striving for effective and transparent communication, thus facilitating collaboration between team members and ensuring that everyone is aligned with the project's objectives.

          📊 CSD - Understanding

          During meetings with sales staff who have been with the company for almost a decade, the ux team and the product team, the certainties were the guaranteed information and feelings that users have.

          Doubts were uncertainties to be resolved that the customer or future customer has towards the software.

          Assumptions are predictions based on previous experiences, such as: at trade fairs where the product is sold, what were the biggest questions future customers had before purchasing the software. This way we can mitigate risks and make more informed decisions during the planning and execution of the project in question.

          👷🏻‍♂️ Who uses?

          Mining Engineers, Geologists, Mining Technicians and Operations Managers are among the professionals who rely on blasting software for their activities.

          The user journey of this software involves an integration of planning, execution, analysis and collaboration, ensuring the success of operations in open-pit mines.

          The use of blasting software plays a crucial role at every stage of the process.

          👣 The Journey

          The user journey when using blasting software involves several essential steps for the safe planning and execution of mining operations. Such as importing topographical data of the area of interest, defining the location and depth of the drill holes where the explosives will be inserted, explosive charge in each drill hole, specifying the type of explosive, quantity and charge pattern to optimize blasting effectiveness.

          ⏱ Timing

          One of the most important crucial points is undoubtedly Timing, which is where the user can predict what will happen, and this process needs a great deal of error prevention.

          Timing involves controlling the time between detonations by programmable electronic devices, such as electronic detonators, allowing precise synchronization and fine adjustments, or by mechanical or non-electronic devices, such as safety fuses or mechanical initiation systems, which is less precise than electronic timing.

          🧪 Simulating

          With the software, the user can simulate the speed of dismantling, allowing a detailed and precise analysis of the explosion process in different scenarios and conditions.

          This functionality gives the user the ability to adjust and optimize the blasting speed to meet the specific needs of the project, taking into account factors such as the type of explosive, the depth of the drill holes, the geological characteristics of the terrain and safety requirements.

          By simulating the blasting speed, the user can identify potential problems or areas for improvement, make informed decisions and ensure efficient and safe execution of blasting operations.
